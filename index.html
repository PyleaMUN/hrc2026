Human Rights Council

Model United Nations Conference

1. Introduction

The United Nations Human Rights Council (HRC) committee in a Model UN simulates the work of the HRC, responsible for promoting and protecting human rights globally through dialogue, monitoring, and resolution adoption.

2. Mandate & Objectives

  • Promotion of Human Rights: Advocate for universal respect for all human rights and fundamental freedoms.
  • Monitoring & Reporting: Conduct country-specific reviews and special procedures investigations.
  • Standard-Setting: Develop international human rights norms and guidelines.
  • Technical Assistance: Provide capacity-building to states and civil society.

3. Committee Structure

  • Membership: 47 member states elected by the General Assembly for three-year terms.
  • Dais: Chair, Vice Chairs.

4. Typical Session Flow

  1. Roll Call & Agenda Adoption
  2. General Debate
  3. Interactive Dialogues: With UN experts, mandate-holders, or country delegations.
  4. Caucusing: Formal and informal negotiations.
  5. Drafting Resolutions

5. Draft Resolution Components

  • Preambulatory Clauses: Reference the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, past HRC resolutions, and international treaties.
  • Operative Clauses: Actions to be taken, monitoring mechanisms, requested reports, and timelines.
